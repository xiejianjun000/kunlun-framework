# Six Thinking Hats

A powerful parallel thinking framework developed by Edward de Bono that enables groups and individuals to think more effectively by separating thinking into six distinct modes.

## Purpose

The Six Thinking Hats method prevents confusion, helps groups think together more effectively, and reduces conflict by separating different types of thinking. Each hat represents a different perspective or thinking style, allowing participants to explore all aspects of a situation without ego conflicts or adversarial positioning.

## The Six Hats

### White Hat - Facts & Information
- Focus: Data, facts, figures, information
- Questions: What do we know? What do we need to know? What information is missing?
- Neutral and objective thinking
- No interpretations or opinions

### Red Hat - Emotions & Feelings
- Focus: Intuition, feelings, emotions, hunches
- Questions: How do I feel about this? What's my gut reaction?
- No justification required
- Legitimizes emotional responses

### Black Hat - Caution & Risk
- Focus: Critical judgment, risks, problems, potential failures
- Questions: What could go wrong? What are the risks? Why might this not work?
- Logical negative assessment
- Essential for contingency planning

### Yellow Hat - Benefits & Value
- Focus: Optimism, benefits, value, feasibility
- Questions: What are the benefits? Why will this work? What's the value proposition?
- Constructive and speculative
- Seeks opportunities

### Green Hat - Creativity & Alternatives
- Focus: New ideas, possibilities, alternatives, creative solutions
- Questions: What are alternatives? How could we do this differently? What if...?
- Lateral thinking
- No criticism allowed

### Blue Hat - Process & Control
- Focus: Managing the thinking process, organization, control
- Questions: What's our agenda? Which hat should we use next? How should we proceed?
- Meta-cognitive thinking
- The conductor's role

## Usage

### Sequential Hat Sequences

**Initial Idea Exploration:**
1. Blue - Define the problem and set agenda
2. White - Gather available facts
3. Green - Generate ideas and alternatives
4. Yellow - Assess benefits of best ideas
5. Black - Assess risks and concerns
6. Green - Develop solutions to address Black Hat concerns
7. Blue - Summarize and plan next steps

**Decision Making:**
1. Blue - Clarify the decision needed
2. White - Present relevant data
3. Green - Generate alternatives
4. Yellow - Benefits of each option
5. Black - Risks of each option
6. Red - Gut feelings on options
7. Blue - Make decision

**Problem Solving:**
1. Blue - Define the problem
2. Red - Initial feelings about the problem
3. White - Gather facts
4. Green - Generate solutions
5. Black - Test each solution
6. Green - Refine solutions
7. Blue - Action plan

**Evaluation:**
1. White - Present the situation
2. Yellow - All benefits
3. Black - All concerns
4. Red - Feelings after balanced view
5. Blue - Conclusion

### Individual Use

When thinking alone, you can:
- Use hats sequentially to structure your thinking
- "Put on" different hats consciously
- Avoid being stuck in one thinking mode
- Ensure balanced consideration

### Team Use

When facilitating groups:
1. Start with Blue hat to set the agenda
2. Have everyone wear the same hat at the same time (parallel thinking)
3. Rotate through hats systematically
4. Use Blue hat to manage transitions and time
5. End with Blue hat for conclusions and action items

## Instructions

1. **Start with Blue Hat**: Always begin by defining the thinking task and establishing which hat sequence to use.

2. **Wear One Hat at a Time**: Focus exclusively on one thinking mode. Don't mix perspectives.

3. **Time Each Hat Session**: Set strict time limits (3-5 minutes per hat in brainstorming).

4. **Honor the Hat Rules**:
   - White: Only facts, no opinions
   - Red: Feelings only, no justification needed
   - Black: Logical negatives only
   - Yellow: Logical positives only
   - Green: Ideas only, no criticism
   - Blue: Process management

5. **Use Blue for Transitions**: Always use Blue Hat when switching between hats or ending the session.

6. **Document Per Hat**: Capture thoughts separately for each hat to maintain clarity.

## Common Sequences

| Purpose | Sequence |
|---------|----------|
| Brainstorming | Blue → White → Green → Yellow → Black → Green → Blue |
| Quick Decision | Blue → White → Green → Red → Blue |
| Feedback | White → Yellow → Black → Red → Blue |
| Problem Analysis | Blue → White → Red → Black → Green → Blue |
| Strategic Planning | Blue → White → Green → Yellow → Black → Red → Blue |

## Tips for Success

- **Enforce parallel thinking**: Everyone thinks in the same direction at the same time
- **Don't skip hats**: Each hat provides unique value
- **Use Red Hat early**: Emotional buy-in matters
- **Balance Black and Yellow**: Ensure equal time for positive and negative
- **Green needs safety**: Create safe environment for wild ideas
- **BlueHat leadership**: Assign someone to manage the process

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Mixing multiple hats in one statement
- Spending too much time on one hat
- Skipping the "uncomfortable" hats (often Red or Green)
- Using Black Hat too early (kills creativity)
- Forgetting to use Blue Hat for process control
- Allowing ego-driven adversarial thinking
