# SWOT Analysis

A strategic planning framework that evaluates situations through four dimensions—Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats—providing a comprehensive view of internal capabilities and external environment.

## Purpose

SWOT Analysis structures complex strategic decisions by separating internal factors (Strengths and Weaknesses) from external factors (Opportunities and Threats). This framework helps organizations and individuals make informed decisions by leveraging capabilities, addressing limitations, capitalizing on possibilities, and mitigating risks.

Developed at Stanford Research Institute in the 1960s, SWOT remains one of the most widely used strategic tools due to its simplicity and effectiveness.

## The Four Dimensions

### Strengths (S) - Internal, Positive

**Characteristics of strengths:**
- Internal attributes that give advantage
- Resources and capabilities
- Things you do well
- Unique assets or positions
- Competitive advantages

**Questions to identify strengths:**
- What advantages does your organization have?
- What do you do better than anyone else?
- What unique resources can you draw on?
- What do others see as your strengths?
- What are your unique selling points?

**Examples:**
- Strong brand recognition
- Proprietary technology
- Talented team
- Financial resources
- Market position
- Customer loyalty
- Operational excellence

### Weaknesses (W) - Internal, Negative

**Characteristics of weaknesses:**
- Internal attributes that put you at disadvantage
- Areas needing improvement
- Resource limitations
- Things competitors do better
- Vulnerabilities

**Questions to identify weaknesses:**
- What could you improve?
- What do you do poorly?
- What should you avoid?
- What resources are you lacking?
- What are your negative reputation factors?
- Where are you vulnerable?

**Examples:**
- Limited budget
- Lack of expertise in key areas
- Weak brand awareness
- Poor location
- Outdated technology
- High costs
- Limited distribution

### Opportunities (O) - External, Positive

**Characteristics of opportunities:**
- External possibilities for growth
- Favorable trends
- Market developments
- Competitive openings
- Environmental factors that can be leveraged

**Questions to identify opportunities:**
- What opportunities are open to you?
- What trends could you take advantage of?
- What changes in the market favor you?
- What competitors are vulnerable?
- What political/social changes help you?
- What technologies create new possibilities?

**Examples:**
- Market growth
- Competitor struggles
- New technologies
- Regulatory changes
- Changing customer preferences
- Strategic partnerships
- New market segments

### Threats (T) - External, Negative

**Characteristics of threats:**
- External challenges
- Risks and uncertainties
- Competitive pressures
- Unfavorable trends
- Environmental dangers

**Questions to identify threats:**
- What threats face your organization?
- What are competitors doing?
- What changing market conditions hurt you?
- What obstacles do you face?
- What substitutes exist for your products?
- Could your weaknesses become critical?

**Examples:**
- New competitors
- Price competition
- Changing regulations
- Economic downturns
- Technological disruption
- Supply chain issues
- Changing customer needs

## Usage

### Strategic Planning

```
Current Situation Analysis:
  Internal (S + W): Our capabilities and limitations
    ↓
  External (O + T): Environment we operate in
    ↓
  Strategy Formulation:
    Build on S
    Address W
    Exploit O
    Mitigate T
```

### Personal Career Planning

```
Self-Assessment:
  Strengths: Skills, experience, qualities
  Weaknesses: Gaps, areas to improve
  Opportunities: Market trends, openings
  Threats: Competition, industry changes
    ↓
  Career Strategy: Where to focus, what to develop
```

### Product Development

```
Market Analysis:
  Internal: Our capabilities in this market
  External: Market conditions and competition
    ↓
  Product Strategy: Features, positioning, timing
```

### Competitive Analysis

```
Competitor SWOT:
  Understand their S, W, O, T
    ↓
  Our Response: How to compete effectively
```

### Project Evaluation

```
Project Assessment:
  Internal: Team, resources, capabilities
  External: Market, timing, environment
    ↓
  Go/No-Go Decision: Is project viable?
```

## Instructions

### Conducting a SWOT Analysis

**Phase 1: Preparation**

1. Define the purpose clearly
2. Assemble diverse perspectives
3. Gather relevant data
4. Set time boundaries (e.g., next 12 months)

**Phase 2: Brainstorming**

1. Create four quadrants on a surface
2. Brainstorm each dimension separately
3. Generate many items initially
4. Be honest and realistic
5. Avoid sugar-coating weaknesses

**Phase 3: Analysis and Prioritization**

1. Combine similar items
2. Identify the most significant items in each quadrant
3. Consider impact vs. controllability
4. Note relationships between items
5. Highlight critical items

**Phase 4: Strategy Development**

1. Generate strategic options
2. Match strengths with opportunities
3. Address weaknesses that exploit opportunities
4. Defend against threats
5. Develop action plans

### Strategic Combinations

**S-O Strategies (Max-Max):**
- Use strengths to capitalize on opportunities
- Leverage capabilities to exploit possibilities
- "Build on strengths to seize opportunities"

**W-O Strategies (Min-Max):**
- Overcome weaknesses to pursue opportunities
- Address limitations to exploit possibilities
- "Fix weaknesses to capture opportunities"

**S-T Strategies (Max-Min):**
- Use strengths to minimize threats
- Leverage capabilities to defend against risks
- "Use strengths to ward off threats"

**W-T Strategies (Min-Min):**
- Minimize weaknesses and avoid threats
- Defensive strategies to reduce vulnerability
- "Fix weaknesses to prepare for threats"

## TOWS Matrix

The TOWS Matrix extends SWOT by systematically developing strategies:

```
           | Opportunities | Threats
-----------|---------------|--------
Strengths  | S-O Strategies| S-T Strategies
           | (Leverage)    | (Defend)
-----------|---------------|--------
Weaknesses | W-O Strategies| W-T Strategies
           | (Improve)     | (Avoid)
```

**S-O Strategies:** How can we use our strengths to take advantage of opportunities?

**W-O Strategies:** How can we overcome weaknesses to pursue opportunities?

**S-T Strategies:** How can we use strengths to reduce or avoid threats?

**W-T Strategies:** How can we minimize weaknesses to avoid threats?

## Tips for Success

- **Be specific and concrete** - Vague statements lead to vague strategies
- **Focus on the most significant** - Not every item is equally important
- **Challenge each item** - Is it really a strength? Is it truly a threat?
- **Be honest** - Denial about weaknesses leads to failure
- **Consider the future** - Not just current state but trends
- **Use data** - Support analysis with facts where possible
- **Involve multiple perspectives** - Diverse viewpoints improve analysis
- **Prioritize** - Focus on 3-5 items per quadrant

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Creating long, unfocused lists
- Confusing internal and external factors
- Being too generic (e.g., "good people" is not specific)
- Confirming what you already believe
- Making strengths out of opportunities (keep them separate)
- Ignoring the analysis once complete
- Failing to develop strategies from findings
- Not updating regularly

## When to Use SWOT

**Ideal for:**
- Strategic planning processes
- New business or product evaluation
- Competitive analysis
- Personal career planning
- Major decision-making
- Organizational change initiatives
- Market entry decisions
- Risk assessment

**Not ideal for:**
- Quick decisions (takes time to do well)
- Highly dynamic situations (rapidly becomes outdated)
- Situations requiring precise quantification
- Simple, straightforward problems

## Integration with Other Tools

**With PESTLE Analysis:**
- Use PESTLE to populate Opportunities and Threats
- PESTLE provides structured external analysis

**With Porter's Five Forces:**
- Use Five Forces to deepen Threats analysis
- Competitive pressures, supplier power, etc.

**With Core Competency Analysis:**
- Use to identify and verify Strengths
- Ensure true competitive advantages

**With Scenario Planning:**
- Use SWOT as baseline for scenario development
- Different scenarios may emphasize different elements
